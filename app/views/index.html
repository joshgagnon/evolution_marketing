OUR PHILOSOPY

With most things in life, you get what you pay for.  But when it comes to legal services, most of the time it’s not exactly clear what you’re ‘getting’.  It’s even less clear what distinguishes one law firm from another.  Practically every law firm out there claims to offer services that are ‘client-focused’, ‘cost-effective’, ‘commercial’, and so on. And generally similar sized firms tend to charge similar rates.  So how do you choose between them?  Office swankiness?  Nice coffee?  How much you like the brand?

This goes right to the top of the profession.  Have a look at what the three top firms in New Zealand say about themselves:

“Legal guides and directories consistently recognise Bell Gully as being New Zealand's leading firm.”
“Russell McVeagh is New Zealand's premier commercial law firm.”
“Chapman Tripp is New Zealand's leading full service law firm with offices in Auckland, Wellington and Christchurch.”

Translation:

"I’m the best”
“No, I’m the best”
“No way, I’m the best”

These three firms are experiencing what we like to call the 'Apple vs. Google phenomenon'.  Apple and Google are similar in size, offer a very similar product offering, and market to a very similar customer base. So what distinguishes Apple from Google? In other words, where is the real competition between them?  It lies, almost exclusively, in the customer experience.  The same applies to law firms that provide essentially the same services.  They are competing on customer experience alone.  Hence the swanky office and nice coffee.

Evolution is different.  We have no premises.  Our lawyers work from home offices with remote supervision from Tamina and Thomas, and remote administration and research support.  This is not only a massive cost saving for the firm; it means our lawyers can be more efficient, productive, and (gasp) happy.  That means better quality services for our clients and much lower staff attrition.  All of this is aimed at creating and fostering long-term client relationships.

We don’t claim to be cost-effective while operating out of a CBD skyscraper in one of most expensive cities in the world
We don’t claim to be modern and innovative while browsing the web with Internet Explorer 8

Having no central premises has absolutely no impact on the quality of the legal product.  Our lawyers are connected to each other 24/7, are subject to the same rules that would apply in any physical law firm, including best practice rules around management, delegation, and peer-review, and make use of all the benefits of modern technology.  

And we mean modern technology.  Tamina and Thomas are the founders of CataLex (www.catalex.nz) – a company at the cutting edge of legal solutions software.  Evolution benefits from its association with CataLex in a number of ways, including by having CataLex develop an online client relationship portal specifically for Evolution.  It also means we can ensure our services are truly modern, and of the highest possible standard.

Despite all this talk about technology, we are not a so-called ‘online law firm’.  You can actually meet us in person, and we encourage that.  We’re happy to come to you, meet at a nice café, or book a meeting or conference rooms at a convenient location.  Whatever works.  We just think, for the most part, email, text, phone, and Skype do the trick just fine.  They are not only convenient; they save cost and mean we can be more available to our clients. 

We don’t claim to be a full service law firm while also claiming to be a firm of specialists
We don’t claim to be a full service law firm while also claiming to be a firm of specialists

Evolution is a boutique firm.  Our lawyers are true specialists.  We provide transactional and litigation services to SMEs in the areas of insolvency, commercial, property, and company law.  That’s it.  If you need something outside of those areas, we will ensure you have access to our extensive referral network, and can assist with managing your instruction.  Simply put, we will never take on an instruction unless we are confident it is within our area of expertise.  For us, it is not about collecting all the work we can; it’s about ensuring all the work we do is of the highest possible standard.

Let’s talk fees.  Most firms charge by the hour – so do we.  The difference?  We charge different rates according to the service being provided; not the person providing the service.  There are three tiers: Category A ($350) – Core Legal Services, for example appearing in Court or drafting a bespoke contract; Category B ($250) – Related Legal Services, for example researching a point of law or corresponding with clients about matter progress; and Category C ($150) – Incidental Services, for example maintaining a data room or preparing a bundle of documents.  

We believe staggering our rates this way not only promotes efficiency; it also makes for better fee estimates and ensures our fees are reasonable, as required by the Lawyers and Conveyancers Act (Lawyers: Conduct and Client Care) Rules 2008.
