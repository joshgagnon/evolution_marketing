OUR PHILOSOPHY

We believe the way lawyers work will change more in the next 50 years than it has in the last 500. With technology advancing faster than ever before, even a profession as old and conservative as law will be forced to adapt. Our philosophy is that embracing this evolution is better than reacting to it. In the end, it’s about ensuring we can always provide the legal services that modern clients require.

Unlike most firms, Evolution Lawyers does not have a central office.  Our lawyers work from home offices with remote supervision from Tamina and Thomas, and remote administration and research support.  This is not only a massive cost saving for the firm; it means our lawyers can be more efficient, productive, and (gasp) happy.  That means better quality services for our clients and much lower staff attrition.  All of this is aimed at creating and fostering long-term client relationships.

We don’t claim to be cost-effective while operating out of a CBD skyscraper in one of the most expensive cities in the world
We don’t claim to be modern and innovative while browsing the web with software that hasn't been updated in a decade

Having no central premises has absolutely no negative impact on the quality of the legal product.  Our lawyers are connected to each other 24/7, are subject to the same rules that would apply in any physical law firm, including best practice rules around management, delegation, and peer-review, and make use of all the benefits of modern technology.  

And we mean modern technology.  Tamina and Thomas are the founders of CataLex – a company at the cutting edge of legal solutions software.  Evolution benefits from its association with CataLex in a number of ways, including by having CataLex develop an online client relationship portal specifically for Evolution.  It also means we can ensure our services are truly modern, and of the highest possible standard.

Despite all this talk about technology, we are not a so-called ‘online law firm’.  You can actually meet us in person, and we encourage that.  We’re happy to come to you, meet at a nice café, or book a meeting or conference room at a convenient location.  Whatever works.  We just think, for the most part, email, text, phone, and Skype do the trick just fine.  They are not only convenient; they save cost and mean we can be more available to our clients. 

We don’t claim to be a full service law firm while also claiming to be a firm of specialists
We don't use a billing system with in-built incentives for inefficiency while claiming to be efficient

Evolution is a boutique firm.  Our lawyers are true specialists.  We provide transactional and litigation services to SMEs in the areas of insolvency, commercial, property, and company law.  That’s it.  If you need something outside of those areas, we will ensure you have access to our extensive referral network, and can assist with managing your instruction.  Simply put, we will never take on an instruction unless we are confident it is within our area of expertise.  For us, it is not about collecting all the work we can; it’s about making sure all the work we do is of the highest possible standard.

Our time isn't worth anything. But our services are.

Let’s talk fees.  Most firms charge by the hour – so do we.  The difference?  We charge different rates according to the service being provided; not the person providing the service.  There are three categories, as per the following table:

Fee category Rate Types of work
A - Core Legal Services $350 Original drafting of instruments and claims, writing a legal opinion, providing written legal advice, providing substantive legal advice over the telephone, Skype, or in person, appearing in Court, acting in a negotiation or mediation, and attending a meeting at your request.
B - Related Legal Services $250 Reviewing documents and evidence, researching the law, considering application of the law, completing a basic precedent document, and correspondence with you, other lawyers, and the Court, including reporting on matter progress, seeking further instructions, and following up deadlines and other known requirements.
C - Incidental Services $150 Proof-reading, compiling a bundle of documents or authorities, administering a data room, and general matter management.
We believe staggering our rates this way not only promotes efficiency; it also makes for better fee estimates and ensures our fees are reasonable, as required by the Lawyers and Conveyancers Act (Lawyers: Conduct and Client Care) Rules 2008.
